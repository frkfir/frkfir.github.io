CoHub

Web app for booking and managing rooms in shared workspaces, with real-time availability and smart filters.

OVERVIEW

CoHub is a responsive web app that simplifies booking and managing rooms in open spaces like coworking hubs and innovation centers. Users can browse room types, check real-time availability, and reserve spaces for meetings or workshops. Designed for both regular and occasional users, CoHub ensures easy access, transparency, and efficient use of shared resources.

THE CHALLENGE

Open workspaces are dynamic environments with multiple room types — meeting rooms, focus pods, phone booths, lounges, etc.
Managing these resources is often chaotic:

  • Employees or visitors don’t know what rooms are available.
  • Booking is typically manual or managed across fragmented systems.
  • Room types and capacities aren’t clearly communicated.
  • Walk-ins disrupt already booked sessions due to unclear signage or real-time updates.

CoHub aimed to solve these pain points with a centralized, user-friendly system that shows room availability, allows bookings, and helps users explore the best fit for their needs without friction.

  • Frustration with double-booked or misused spaces.

Noa needs quiet rooms for focused design critiques and collaborative spaces for team brainstorms. She wants to see real photos, room layouts, and available equipment before booking. Since her schedule is flexible, real-time availability and mobile booking are essential for her workflow.

  • Hard to track how often certain rooms are booked vs. idle.

DATA ANALYSIS

Before designing CoHub, we conducted research using:

  • Surveys and Interviews with 25 users across 3 coworking spaces.
  • Shadowing users during booking and room-hunting processes.
  • Analysis of booking calendars, Excel logs, and whiteboard usage trackers.

Key Findings:

Unexpected Occupancy

68 %

Users attempted to use a room but found it occupied despite appearing “free.”

Need for Previews

84 %

users wanted to preview a room’s amenities (e.g., screens, capacity).

Equipment Uncertainty

58 %

Felt unsure about what each room was actually equipped with.

THE SOLUTION

CoHub is a responsive web app that shows real-time room availability, lets users filter by type and amenities, and offers quick, clear booking with room details and photos. The solution involved advanced AI tools to optimize recommendations, enhance search precision, and improve user experience. It includes an admin dashboard for tracking usage and supports accessibility through keyboard and ARIA features.

Amud Aanan

(Redesign)

Israeli-focused digital topographic map and geographical encyclopedia, free for users. Key audience: hikers, students, travelers, lovers of the Land of Israel.

OVERVIEW

Amud Aanan present maps of the Land of Israel and create an online geographical encyclopedia that will be accessible to the browsing public, free of charge. All this is aimed at being a tool for travelers, students, and lovers of the Land of Israel.

THE CHALLENGE

The current interface is visually outdated and lacks clarity, with buttons and icons that are often hard to see or understand—especially in outdoor use. Both the mobile and desktop versions suffer from inconsistent layouts and interactions, making the experience confusing for users switching between platforms.
Core tools like accessing nearby springs, parking, or importing GPX files are not easily accessible from the home screen, leading to inefficient user flows. The lack of a clear visual hierarchy and absence of onboarding or helpful guidance further contributes to user frustration, especially for first-time users.

A redesign is essential to improve visibility, streamline interactions, unify the platform experience, and simplify access to key features.
